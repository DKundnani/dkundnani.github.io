About me

During my Ph.D. journey at GeorgiaTech, my focus has been on discovery and validation of ribonucleotide incorporation in human genome as a novel epigenomics phenomena. I have also discovered unique ribonucleotide embedment features in associated rare genetic disorder mutants, studied enzyme associations in large public datasets and developed tailored software packages. For the same work, I have been awarded Mark Borodovsky Prize for Excellence in Bioinformatics at Georgia Institute of Technology.
Before coming to Georgia Tech, I have developed, optimized and validated multiplex Immuno Assays for protein biomarkers in patient blood samples for early detection of multiple cancers, fortunate to have worked with amazing doctors and scientists at Hanash Lab in MD Anderson Cancer Center. The incredible effort in diagnostics of Lung Cancer Risk Assessment Biomarkers has been recognized by the institution.
